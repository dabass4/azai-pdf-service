## üéØ Overview

### What are HCPCS Codes?

**HCPCS** (Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System) codes are used to bill Medicare and Medicaid for healthcare services and supplies. For home health agencies, these codes determine:

- What services can be billed
- How services are measured (per visit, per 15 minutes, per day)
- Reimbursement rates
- Prior authorization requirements

### Code Categories

- **G Codes:** Medicare/Medicaid services (e.g., G0156)
- **T Codes:** State Medicaid programs (e.g., T1019)
- **S Codes:** Private payers, some state Medicaid (e.g., S5125)
- **H Codes:** Mental health, substance abuse (e.g., H0038)

## üè• Personal Care Services

### T1019 - Personal Care Services
**Description:** Personal care services, per 15 minutes  
**Use:** Assistance with Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)  
**Billing Unit:** 15 minutes  
**Common Services:**
- Bathing
- Dressing
- Grooming
- Feeding assistance
- Toileting assistance
- Ambulation support

**Example:**
- 2 hours of personal care = 8 units (120 minutes √∑ 15 = 8)

**Payer Notes:**
- Widely accepted by state Medicaid waiver programs
- May require prior authorization
- Documentation must specify ADL assistance provided

---

### T1020 - Personal Care Services (Per Diem)
**Description:** Personal care services, per diem (daily rate)  
**Use:** Daily rate for personal care services not provided by CNA/HHA  
**Billing Unit:** Per day  
**Common Services:**
- Full-day personal care
- 24-hour care arrangements
- Extended home support

**Restrictions:**
- Patient cannot be in nursing facility or hospital
- Not for services provided by certified nurse aides
- State-specific coverage

## üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è Home Health Aide Services

### G0156 - Home Health Aide Services
**Description:** Services of a home health aide in home health or hospice settings  
**Use:** Skilled home health aide services under plan of care  
**Billing Unit:** 15 minutes  
**Common Services:**
- Personal care by certified aide
- Bathing and hygiene
- Simple dressing changes
- Vital sign monitoring
- Medication reminders
- Light housekeeping related to patient care

**Example:**
- 1.5 hours of aide services = 6 units (90 minutes √∑ 15 = 6)

**Requirements:**
- Must be under home health plan of care
- Aide must be certified (HHA or CNA)
- Requires physician order
- Medicare and Medicaid accepted

**CMS Guidelines:**
- Documented in 15-minute increments
- Must be medically necessary
- Supervision requirements apply

## üè° Homemaker & Companion Services

### S5125 - Homemaker Service
**Description:** Homemaker services, per 15 minutes  
**Use:** Assistance with household tasks  
**Billing Unit:** 15 minutes  
**Common Services:**
- Light housekeeping
- Meal preparation
- Laundry
- Shopping assistance
- Basic cleaning

**Example:**
- 2 hours of homemaker services = 8 units

**Payer Coverage:**
- Common in Medicaid waiver programs
- May not be covered by traditional Medicare
- State-specific variations

---

### S5126 - Companion Service
**Description:** Companion services, per 15 minutes  
**Use:** Non-medical support and supervision  
**Billing Unit:** 15 minutes  
**Common Services:**
- Companionship
- Supervision for safety
- Social interaction
- Light activities
- Monitoring (non-medical)

**Example:**
- 4 hours of companion services = 16 units

**Important Notes:**
- Non-skilled service
- Does NOT include hands-on care
- Cannot include ADL assistance (use T1019 instead)

## üíâ Skilled Nursing Services

### G0299 - Direct Skilled Nursing Services (RN)
**Description:** Direct skilled nursing services of a registered nurse (RN) in home health or hospice  
**Use:** Skilled nursing visits  
**Billing Unit:** 15 minutes  
**Common Services:**
- Wound care
- IV therapy
- Injection administration
- Patient assessment
- Disease management
- Patient/caregiver education

**Example:**
- 1 hour nursing visit = 4 units

---

### G0300 - Direct Skilled Nursing Services (LPN)
**Description:** Direct skilled nursing services of a licensed practical nurse (LPN)  
**Use:** Skilled nursing by LPN  
**Billing Unit:** 15 minutes  
**Common Services:**
- Wound care (simple)
- Medication administration
- Catheter care
- Vital signs monitoring
- Patient assessment (under RN supervision)

**Note:** LPN services must be supervised by RN per state regulations

## üõå Respite Care

### S5150 - Unskilled Respite Care (Per Diem)
**Description:** Unskilled respite care, per diem  
**Use:** Temporary relief for primary caregiver  
**Billing Unit:** Per day  
**Common Services:**
- Short-term care provision
- Caregiver relief
- Basic supervision
- Personal care

**Example:**
- 24 hours of respite care = 1 unit (per day)

**Medicaid Waiver Coverage:**
- Common in HCBS waivers
- Prior authorization often required
- Annual hour limits may apply

---

### S5151 - Unskilled Respite Care (Per Hour)
**Description:** Unskilled respite care, per hour  
**Use:** Hourly respite care services  
**Billing Unit:** Per hour  

**Example:**
- 4 hours of respite = 4 units

## üìù Documentation Requirements

### For All Home Health Services:

**Required Documentation:**
1. **Physician Order** - Signed and dated
2. **Plan of Care** - Detailed service plan
3. **Assessment** - Initial and ongoing
4. **Progress Notes** - Each visit documented
5. **Time Logs** - Start/end times
6. **Service Description** - What was provided
7. **Patient Signature** - Service verification (or caregiver if patient unable)

### Ohio-Specific Requirements:

**Electronic Visit Verification (EVV):**
- **Required for:** Personal care services
- **Must Capture:**
  - Type of service
  - Individual receiving service
  - Individual providing service
  - Date and time (start/end)
  - Location (GPS coordinates)
  - Signature

**EVV Mandate:**
- Federal requirement (21st Century Cures Act)
- Ohio implementation: Ongoing
- Applies to all personal care and home health aide services

## üö´ Non-Covered Services

**Generally NOT covered by Ohio Medicaid:**
- General housekeeping (beyond patient-related)
- Shopping (unless directly related to patient care)
- Transportation (separate codes apply)
- Pet care
- Yard work
- General errands

**Verify Coverage:**
- Always check specific managed care plan policies
- Review Ohio Medicaid bulletins
- Confirm with payer before providing service
